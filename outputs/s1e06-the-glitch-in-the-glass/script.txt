The Glitch in the Glass — Ep 6: A Desperate Tap

NARRATOR: Previously on The Glitch in the Glass: Sam's research unveiled chilling folklore surrounding doorway mirrors. Their reflections, now gaunt and desperate, were trapped in a desolate version of their world, a reflected door standing ajar.
CHLOE: I can’t stop thinking about what Sam found. The doorway mirrors. The soul traps.
LIAM: It’s just old stories, Chloe. Someone made them up a long time ago.
SAM: But the reflections… they’re real. I looked again this morning. My reflection… it looks so tired, so afraid.
MAYA: Mine too. Like it hasn't slept in weeks. And that door in the reflection… it’s wider now.
NARRATOR: As Maya spoke, they all looked toward the antique mirror in her dusty attic bedroom. The reflected door, once ajar, now gaped a little more, revealing only deeper shadow within.
LIAM: Okay, fine. It's creepy. But it’s still just a reflection. It can’t actually… do anything.
NARRATOR: Just as Liam finished his sentence, his reflection in the mirror moved. Not just mirroring his expression, but deliberately, slowly, it raised its reflected hand.
CHLOE: Liam… look!
NARRATOR: The reflected hand pressed flat against the glass, then tapped, once. A faint, almost imperceptible sound, yet undeniably there, echoing in the quiet room.
LIAM: What the… No. That’s not possible.
SAM: It’s trying to communicate. Like it’s trapped on the other side.
MAYA: And it’s trying to get out. Or… get in.
NARRATOR: A cold, invisible dread filled the room, a pressure that seemed to push them closer to the shimmering surface. The air felt heavy, as if their own reality was thinning, stretching taut.
CHLOE: I feel… dizzy. Like the room is tilting.
SAM: It’s like the mirror is calling us. Drawing us in.
LIAM: We need to cover it. Now. Before it does something else.
MAYA: It won’t help, Liam. I feel it. It’s already here. And it’s getting stronger.
