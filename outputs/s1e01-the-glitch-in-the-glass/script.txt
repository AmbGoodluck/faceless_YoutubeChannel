The Glitch in the Glass — Ep 1: The Mirror's Secret Reflection

NARRATOR: The old Victorian house had been in Maya’s family for generations, accumulating more than just dust in its shadowy corners. When her grandmother passed, a final, peculiar item was listed in the will: a mirror from the late 1800s, described only as 'The Looking Glass of Whispers.'
MAYA: Honestly, I don't know where Grandma even kept this thing. It's huge. And heavy.
LIAM: Seriously. My back is going to sue me. Are you sure you want this in your bedroom? It looks like something out of a haunted mansion movie.
CHLOE: Oh, I love it! Look at the detail on the frame. It’s magnificent, Maya. Think of the stories it could tell.
SAM: Or the dust bunnies it could collect. Here, a little more to the left, Liam. No, your left.
LIAM: My left is your right, apparently. Just tell me when it's centered.
NARRATOR: After much straining and a few near-misses with the wall, the mirror was finally propped against the far wall of Maya's bedroom, facing her antique four-poster bed.
MAYA: Perfect. Thank you, guys, seriously. I couldn't have done that alone.
CHLOE: It really transforms the room. It feels… grander now.
SAM: And much darker, with that giant black hole sucking up all the light. Anyone else feel like it’s a little… off?
LIAM: Off how? It's just an old mirror. Probably needs a good cleaning.
MAYA: No, Sam's right. Look closely. My lamp… it's not quite where it should be in the reflection.
CHLOE: Oh, you're right! It's shifted slightly to the left. But it's clearly on the right here. That's weird.
LIAM: It's an antique mirror. The glass is probably warped. Or it's a trick of the light. Stand directly in front of it, Maya.
NARRATOR: Maya stepped forward, her reflection appearing instantly. Her friends watched her, then her mirrored self.
MAYA: Okay, now I see it. It's not just the lamp. My hair… I know I just tied it up, but in the reflection, it looks a little… messier. Like I've been running my hand through it.
SAM: And the book on your nightstand. I swear it was open face down. In the mirror, it's closed.
CHLOE: My scarf! I draped it over the chair, but in the reflection, it's on the floor next to it. Just barely.
LIAM: Okay, that's… genuinely strange. This isn't just old glass. It’s like a slightly altered reality.
MAYA: It's subtle, though. If you weren't looking for it, you wouldn't even notice. Like a tiny glitch.
SAM: Maybe it's one of those haunted mirrors, like in the urban legends? They say some can show you glimpses of the past, or even a different present.
CHLOE: Or the future. Imagine if it showed something truly terrifying.
LIAM: Let's not get ahead of ourselves. There's probably a scientific explanation. Refraction, angles, maybe a weird chemical in the silvering. I'll Google 'anomalous antique mirror reflections'.
MAYA: No, wait. Look at my reflection again. My eyes. They just… moved. But I didn't.
NARRATOR: In the mirror, Maya's reflection, distinct and separate, turned its head almost imperceptibly, its gaze lingering on something just out of frame behind her.
CHLOE: Did you see that? It looked… curious.
SAM: Curious about what? There's nothing there.
LIAM: Okay, now that's a trick of the light. Or our minds playing tricks on us. We're all wired now to see things after what Maya said.
MAYA: No, it wasn't. It felt… deliberate. And now, my reflection looks a little… sad. Or maybe worried.
NARRATOR: The four friends stared into the ornate mirror, their own reflections looking back, perfectly normal. But Maya’s, standing directly in front of the glass, seemed to be conveying a different emotion entirely, a quiet anxiety that deepened with each passing second.
LIAM: This is starting to creep me out. Maybe we should cover it for the night. Just until we figure out what's going on.
SAM: Good idea. I’m already getting chills. What if it gets worse when we're asleep?
CHLOE: It feels like it's watching us. Especially Maya.
MAYA: I’ll find an old sheet in the attic. Be right back.
NARRATOR: Maya turned, heading for the bedroom door, her back to the mirror. As she reached the threshold, her reflection in the glass, still facing outward, slowly lifted a hand. It pressed a ghostly palm against the mirrored surface, as if trying to reach out from behind the glass.
