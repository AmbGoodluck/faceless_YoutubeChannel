The Glitch in the Glass — Ep 8: Worlds Collide

NARRATOR: Previously on The Glitch in the Glass: Maya's reflection became a terrifying entity, pleading for release.
LIAM: It’s getting colder in here, has anyone else noticed?
CHLOE: It's not just the temperature. I keep hearing… things. Like whispers, just at the edge of hearing.
MAYA: I thought I was imagining it. And the air… it feels thick, almost fuzzy around the mirror now.
SAM: I’ve been trying to find anything about antique mirrors, dimensional rifts… not much useful beyond urban legends.
CHLOE: Wait. What is that?
NARRATOR: Chloe pointed to the dusty wooden floor directly in front of the mirror. There, resting against the intricate frame, was a single, wilted rosebud. Its petals were a deep, bruised purple, and its stem was dry and brittle.
LIAM: Where did that come from? It wasn’t here a second ago.
MAYA: I don't keep flowers like that in the house. Not even close.
SAM: It looks… ancient. Like it’s been dead for decades.
NARRATOR: As Sam reached out a cautious finger, the rosebud shimmered, its form flickering like an old film projector, and then, in an instant, it was gone. The spot where it lay was empty, only dust remaining.
LIAM: Did anyone else just see that?
CHLOE: It appeared… and then it just vanished.
MAYA: It's happening. The two worlds… they're bleeding into each other.
SAM: If objects can come through… what else can?
NARRATOR: A sudden gust of frigid wind swept through the room, though all windows were closed. The mirror’s surface rippled like disturbed water, and for a fleeting moment, Maya’s reflection was gone, replaced by a gaunt, distorted version of herself, its mouth agape in a silent scream, its eyes wide with a desperate, primal terror.
MAYA: It’s trying to pull us in. I can feel it.
