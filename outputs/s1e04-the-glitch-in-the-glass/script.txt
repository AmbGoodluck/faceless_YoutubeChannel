The Glitch in the Glass — Ep 4: The Mirror That Glows

NARRATOR: Previously, the mirror revealed its unsettling nature, showing not just reflections, but distorted realities, layering them over one another. It absorbed their attempts to change it, and then, a chilling message appeared within the glass.
MAYA: It feels like it’s watching us, even when we’re not looking directly at it.
LIAM: That's just paranoia, Maya. It’s a mirror. It reflects light. It can’t actually 'watch' anything.
CHLOE: I don't know, Liam. I caught my reflection smiling earlier, just for a second. And I definitely wasn't smiling.
SAM: Mine just blinked. Like, out of sync with me. It was weird. Felt… delayed, somehow.
NARRATOR: The friends had gathered once more in Maya's attic bedroom, the mirror now propped against a far wall, facing away from them, but its presence was still palpable. They spoke in hushed tones, the afternoon light filtering weakly through the dusty window.
MAYA: We have to do something. We can’t just let it sit there, doing… whatever it's doing.
LIAM: What do you propose? Smashing it? We already saw what happens when we try to alter it directly. The cracks just… healed.
CHLOE: Maybe we just need to cover it. Completely. Block out the light, block out the… whatever.
SAM: It’s worth a shot. I found some heavy-duty tarps in the garage. And rope. We can really secure it.
NARRATOR: They retrieved the materials. A thick, dark tarp, industrial-grade, was pulled from a storage bin. The air in the attic was heavy with dust and a subtle, metallic scent that seemed to emanate from the mirror itself.
MAYA: Okay, everyone ready? On three. Let’s try to keep it steady.
LIAM: Wait. Let's make sure it's completely dark under there. No gaps.
CHLOE: This feels a bit like trying to put a blanket over a monster.
SAM: Better a blanket than staring into its eyes.
NARRATOR: With a coordinated effort, they lifted the heavy tarp, positioning it carefully over the tall, ornate mirror. It was a struggle, the sheer size of the antique making the task unwieldy. As the final edge settled, a profound darkness enveloped the glass. They then used rope to tightly secure the tarp around the frame, tying knots that would hold against any movement.
MAYA: There. Done. Can't see a thing.
LIAM: Good. Now let's just leave it. Come back in a few days, see if anything feels different.
CHLOE: Are you sure that’s enough? It still feels… present.
SAM: It's the best we can do for now. We've cut off its access to light, to reflections. That has to do something.
NARRATOR: They gathered their things, a cautious sense of relief settling over them. As they turned to leave the room, a faint, pulsing light caught Maya's eye. It wasn't bright, barely visible, but it was unmistakably there. It emanated from beneath the secured tarp, a soft, internal glow, like a trapped firefly.
MAYA: Guys… look.
LIAM: What is it?
CHLOE: It's coming from… under the tarp.
SAM: How? We sealed it. There’s no way light is getting in or out.
NARRATOR: The light pulsed rhythmically, a soft, almost living beat from the covered mirror. It wasn't reflecting anything. It was creating its own light, an impossible glow from deep within its glass, through layers of thick, impenetrable fabric. It seemed to grow brighter, just slightly, with each beat, a silent, defiant pulse against their attempts to contain it.
