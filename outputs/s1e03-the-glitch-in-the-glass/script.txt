The Glitch in the Glass — Ep 3: You're Already Here

NARRATOR: Previously, the friends discovered the antique mirror wasn't just showing their reflections; it was subtly altering reality within its glass, from Chloe's clothes to Liam's monitor. The glitches were becoming more frequent, more personal.
MAYA: It's not just my reflection anymore. It's like... it's showing us a different version of everything.
LIAM: A different version? Like, a parallel universe in silvered glass? Come on, Maya.
CHLOE: He's right, Liam. My cat, Whiskers. He's not in the reflection. He's always on my bed when I'm in my room. But in the mirror? Empty quilt.
SAM: Maybe he just moved. Cats do that.
CHLOE: I checked. He was there, curled up, when I looked away. Looked back at the mirror, still empty. Then I looked at the bed again, and he was still there. It's only in the mirror that he's gone.
MAYA: And the wall by the fireplace... there's a new crack in the reflection. It's faint, but it's definitely not there in real life.
LIAM: Okay, a missing cat and a phantom crack. This is getting weirder. What about my poster reflection?
SAM: Still distorted. I tried to find anything online about mirrors doing this. Ancient curses, haunted artifacts, quantum entanglement... nothing concrete that matches.
MAYA: Maybe we should try to 'correct' it. If the mirror shows something different, what if we change the real thing to match the reflection?
LIAM: Or change the real thing to contradict the reflection, forcing it to snap back?
CHLOE: Let's try Liam's idea first. If it's showing something missing, like Whiskers, what if we put something there that isn't supposed to be?
SAM: Or Maya's idea. The crack in the wall. What if we try to 'fix' the wall, even if there's nothing to fix?
NARRATOR: They gathered in Maya's bedroom, the large antique mirror dominating one wall. Maya pointed to a small, unread paperback resting on her bedside table.
MAYA: That book. 'Whispers in the Dark.' I haven't touched it. But in the mirror, it looks like it's been read. The spine is cracked, pages fanned out.
LIAM: Right. So, to contradict it... what if we put a different book there? One that definitely hasn't been read.
CHLOE: Or one that has been read, but not that one.
MAYA: No, let's keep it simple. Something new. I'll get 'The Midnight Choir' from the shelf. It's brand new, still has the plastic wrap.
NARRATOR: Maya retrieved the new book, its shrink-wrap glinting under the room's soft light. She carefully placed it on the bedside table, right where 'Whispers in the Dark' had been.
SAM: Okay. Now look in the mirror.
NARRATOR: They all leaned forward, peering into the glass. The new book, 'The Midnight Choir', sat on the table. But within the mirror's depth, 'Whispers in the Dark' remained. Its spine still cracked, its pages still fanned open, a silent testament to a reading that hadn't happened in their world.
CHLOE: It's... it's still there. The other book. Even with the new one on the table.
LIAM: No, wait. Look closely. The Midnight Choir is also there, sitting on top of Whispers in the Dark. Like they're both there, simultaneously.
MAYA: It's not trying to match us. It's just adding to itself. Layering realities.
SAM: So, we can't change what it shows by altering our reality. It just incorporates our changes, without deleting its own.
CHLOE: What about the crack in the wall, Maya? Did it... stay there?
NARRATOR: Maya turned, her gaze falling on the mirrored reflection of the wall by the fireplace. The faint, jagged line was still visible, a phantom fissure in plaster that wasn't there in the physical room. But then, as she watched, the crack in the glass seemed to deepen, to darken. A tiny piece of plaster, just within the reflection, seemed to detach, falling away into the mirrored void.
MAYA: It's getting worse. The crack... it's growing. And a piece just fell off. In the reflection.
LIAM: Fell off? How can a reflection lose a piece? It's just light, right?
SAM: This isn't just a reflection, Liam. It's a window. Or a canvas. And it's painting its own picture.
CHLOE: But a canvas doesn't change reality. This mirror... what if it's not just reflecting? What if it's absorbing? Or making these changes... real?
NARRATOR: The air in the room grew heavy, a cold dread seeping into their bones. They exchanged uneasy glances, the implications settling in. The mirror wasn't just showing them another world; it was actively shaping it. And perhaps, shaping theirs too.
MAYA: It's not just showing us a missing cat or a crack. It's building its own world. And what happens when its world gets too real? What happens when it shows something truly... wrong?
LIAM: Wrong how? Like, showing us something terrible?
CHLOE: Like showing us a world where *we* are missing. Or worse.
SAM: We need to figure out what it wants. Before it decides what *it* wants for us.
NARRATOR: As if in response, a new detail shimmered into existence within the glass. Reflected on Maya's bedroom door, where a simple 'Do Not Disturb' sign usually hung, a different message now appeared. It was scrawled, crude, and deeply unsettling.
MAYA: No. No, what is that?
LIAM: Is that... blood?
CHLOE: It says... 'You're already here'.
