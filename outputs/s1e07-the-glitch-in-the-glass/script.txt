The Glitch in the Glass — Ep 7: Trapped in the Reflection

NARRATOR: Previously, Liam’s reflection had tapped on the glass, a desperate attempt to communicate. The friends felt the mirror’s pull, its distorted reality becoming unsettlingly tangible. They stood before it again, the air thick with apprehension.
LIAM: Are we sure about this? It feels… stronger today.
CHLOE: We need to know what it wants. We can’t just ignore it now.
SAM: I’ve been digging online. There are old folklore tales about 'mirror selves' – reflections that gain sentience and try to swap places. They call it the 'Glass Swap'.
MAYA: Swap places? That’s… that’s what Liam’s reflection was doing, wasn’t it? Trying to get out.
NARRATOR: Maya stepped forward, drawn by an unseen force. Her reflection in the antique glass mimicked her perfectly, a flawless echo of her movements. For a few tense seconds, they were indistinguishable.
LIAM: It’s doing it again. Just like before.
CHLOE: Look closely. There’s something… off.
NARRATOR: Then, the reflection's movements diverged. A slight hesitation in its smile, a subtle shift in its posture. Its eyes, Maya’s eyes, widened slightly, a flicker of raw, undisguised terror in their depths. It pressed a ghostly hand against the glass, not mimicking Maya’s touch, but as if trying to reach *out*.
SAM: It’s not just mimicking anymore. It’s… reacting.
MAYA: It’s not me. It’s not me. Get me away from this thing!
NARRATOR: The reflected Maya’s face contorted, her lips forming silent, desperate pleas. Her reflection pounded softly on the inside of the glass, a frantic, rhythmic plea for release. Her real hands flew to her face, shielding her eyes from the horror staring back. The room seemed to twist, the air growing heavy and cold around them. The reflection’s gaze was fixed, not on Maya, but past her, as if seeing something else entirely.
CHLOE: It’s begging. It wants out. And it looks… terrified.
LIAM: Terrified of what? Of us? Or of… something else in there with it?
NARRATOR: The reflected Maya’s eyes, wide and wet, darted nervously to the side, then back to the group. A profound, bone-chilling fear emanated from the glass, not from Maya, but from her imprisoned counterpart. The mirror pulsed with an unseen energy, the reflection’s pleas becoming more desperate, its eyes locking onto Maya’s, begging for an answer only she could give.
