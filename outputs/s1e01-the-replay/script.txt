The Replay — Ep 1: Stuck at 3:47

NARRATOR: Maya Chen had always been the kind of person who noticed things. Small things. The way her best friend Jordan always checked her phone before answering a question. The way her English teacher, Ms. Hartwell, seemed to know what she was going to say before she said it. Things that didn't quite add up. But on Tuesday, October 15th, she noticed something that would change everything.
NARRATOR: It started like any other morning.
MAYA: Okay, okay, I'm up.
NARRATOR: She said to her phone alarm. 6:47 AM. The same time she woke up every day.
MAYA: Mom, I can smell the coffee from here. I know breakfast is ready.
NARRATOR: Downstairs, her mother was making toast. Always toast on Tuesdays.
MAYA'S MOM: You're going to be late. Jordan texted. She's already in the car.
MAYA: It's 7:02. School doesn't start until 8:15. How am I going to be late?
NARRATOR: At Westridge High, the hallways were the same as they always were. The same conversations. Jordan was waiting by their lockers, holding two coffees.
JORDAN: You look tired. Did you even sleep?
MAYA: I slept fine. Why does everyone keep saying that?
NARRATOR: But something felt different today. Not wrong. Just... off. Like a word on the tip of her tongue that she couldn't quite reach.
MAYA: Hey, did we have plans last weekend? Like, something specific?
JORDAN: What? No. We just hung out at your place. Why?
MAYA: I don't know. Just checking.
NARRATOR: First period. English. Ms. Hartwell was writing something on the board when Maya walked in. The teacher turned, and for just a moment, she smiled at Maya. Not a normal smile. A knowing one.
MS. HARTWELL: Maya. Good morning. Take your seat.
NARRATOR: During the lesson, Ms. Hartwell called on her to read a passage aloud. Maya read it. The teacher listened, nodding slightly, as if she'd heard it before.
MAYA: Is that... okay?
MS. HARTWELL: Very good. Exactly as I expected.
NARRATOR: The day moved forward. Lunch. Physics. Chemistry. At 3:14 PM, the fire alarm went off.
NARRATOR: It was loud. Everyone moved to the exits, filing out into the parking lot. The October air was cold. The sky was gray. A delivery truck was idling near the main entrance, its engine running.
JORDAN: This is so annoying. We were supposed to have a quiz.
MAYA: You're relieved.
JORDAN: Completely relieved.
NARRATOR: Maya was standing near the parking lot edge, watching the other students. She noticed the delivery truck driver looking at his phone. Not paying attention. At 3:43 PM, the driver's foot slipped off the brake. The truck rolled forward. Slowly at first. Then faster.
NARRATOR: Maya heard the sound before she felt anything. Metal. Impact. And then, nothing.
NARRATOR: She woke up to her alarm.
MAYA: What...?
NARRATOR: 6:47 AM. Tuesday. October 15th. Same as before.
MAYA: No. No, that's not... I was just...
NARRATOR: She checked her phone. No missed calls. No messages about an accident. She opened the news. Nothing. Her heart was still racing. She could still hear the sound of the truck.
MAYA: It was a dream. It was just a really vivid dream.
NARRATOR: But she went through the day differently this time. She stayed away from the parking lot. She didn't stand near the exit doors. At 3:14 PM, the fire alarm went off again. Same time. Same sound.
MAYA: Jordan, we're not going to the parking lot. We're staying in the building.
JORDAN: What are you talking about? The alarm means we evacuate.
MAYA: Just trust me. Please.
NARRATOR: They went to the gymnasium instead. Waited there. Minutes passed. 3:43 PM came and went. No truck. No impact. Nothing.
MAYA: See? We're fine. We're completely fine.
NARRATOR: The fire department came and cleared the building. The day ended. She went home. She did her homework. She went to bed.
NARRATOR: At 6:47 AM, her alarm went off again.
MAYA: No. No, no, no.
NARRATOR: Same day. Same Tuesday. Same October 15th. This time, Maya didn't go to the gymnasium. She stayed in her first period class. Sat at her desk and watched the clock. Watched Ms. Hartwell, who kept looking at her like she was waiting for something. At 3:14 PM, the fire alarm sounded.
MS. HARTWELL: Everyone to the exits. Maya, I'll see you tomorrow.
MAYA: Ms. Hartwell, what do you mean? Tomorrow is... this is today.
MS. HARTWELL: I know. I'll see you tomorrow anyway.
NARRATOR: That statement landed wrong. Maya ran to the parking lot, even though she knew what would happen. At 3:43 PM, the truck came. This time, she saw it coming. She jumped out of the way. She almost made it. Almost.
NARRATOR: She woke up again. 6:47 AM. Tuesday. October 15th.
NARRATOR: By the seventh loop, Maya had tested seventeen different versions of Tuesday. By the tenth, she had the entire day memorized. Every sentence. Every interaction. Every single person's movement. And there was one detail she couldn't ignore.
NARRATOR: The fire alarm always went off at 3:14 PM. The truck always arrived at 3:43 PM. And no matter what she did, no matter where she went, someone always died at 3:47 PM. Not always her. Sometimes Jordan. Once, she watched Dev Kapoor get hit instead. But someone always died at exactly the same time.
NARRATOR: On the eleventh loop, Maya sat down next to Dev in the cafeteria. He was quiet, usually kept to himself. He looked up, surprised.
DEV: Hey. We don't really know each other.
MAYA: I know. But I need to ask you something. Do you know anything about a countdown?
DEV: What?
MAYA: A countdown. Like, something that happens every day. Every day at the same time.
NARRATOR: Dev went pale. He stood up and walked away. But before he left, he whispered something to her.
DEV: Stop asking questions. You don't want to know.
NARRATOR: That night, on the eleventh loop, Maya couldn't sleep. She was exhausted. She'd lived the same day eleven times. Eleven times she'd seen people die. Eleven times she'd woken up to the same alarm. And now there was a countdown she didn't understand. A countdown that Dev Kapoor knew about. And as she lay in bed, staring at the ceiling, something occurred to her that she hadn't considered before.
NARRATOR: Ms. Hartwell had said she'd see her tomorrow. But they lived the same day. Every single day was today. So when tomorrow came, and it was the same day again, what would Ms. Hartwell say then?
NARRATOR: At 6:47 AM, her alarm went off. The twelfth loop was about to begin. And Maya was no longer just afraid of dying. She was afraid of something much worse.
NARRATOR: She was afraid she would never leave.
